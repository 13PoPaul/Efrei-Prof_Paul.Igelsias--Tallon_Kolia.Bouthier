Mathematics

Discrete Mathematics

📅 Semester 1 • 🎓 5 ECTS Credits • 👨‍🏫 Prof. George Boole

Course Overview

This course provides the mathematical foundations essential for computer science. Students will explore logic, set theory, combinatorics, discrete probability, and boolean algebra, all of which are crucial for designing algorithms and understanding computational complexity.

Learning Objectives

  • Master propositional and predicate logic to evaluate computational statements.
  • Understand and apply the concepts of set theory, relations, and functions.
  • Solve complex counting problems using permutations, combinations, and the pigeonhole principle.
  • Apply boolean algebra principles to digital logic circuit design and software conditions.

Prerequisites

A solid foundation in high school-level algebra and arithmetic is highly recommended.

🧠 Quick Concept Check

Test your intuition before starting the course!

1. Which symbol is used to represent the intersection of two sets A and B?

2. In Boolean algebra, what is the result of the operation "1 AND 0"?
