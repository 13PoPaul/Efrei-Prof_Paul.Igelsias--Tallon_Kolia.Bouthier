Cybersecurity

Applied Cryptography

📅 Semester 4 • 🎓 5 ECTS Credits • 👨‍🏫 Prof. Alice Rivest

Course Overview

This course explores the fundamental principles of data protection and secure communication. Students will study symmetric and asymmetric encryption algorithms, understand the role of hash functions, and learn how digital signatures and certificates ensure authenticity and integrity in modern digital systems.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the mathematical foundations of modern cryptographic systems.
  • Differentiate between symmetric (AES) and asymmetric (RSA, ECC) encryption and their use cases.
  • Implement and analyze cryptographic hash functions (SHA-256) and their applications in data integrity.
  • Understand the mechanics of digital signatures, Public Key Infrastructure (PKI), and secure key exchange protocols.

Prerequisites

A good understanding of discrete mathematics, modular arithmetic, and basic programming skills (Python or C/C++) is required to successfully follow this course.
